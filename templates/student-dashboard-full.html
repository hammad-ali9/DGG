Frequently Asked Questions
When is my application due? ▾
Application deadlines are: Fall — August 1, Winter — December 1, Spring — April 1, Summer — June 1. Late applications require Director approval and are not guaranteed. Always apply before the deadline.
What is Form B and do I need to submit it? ▾
Form B is an enrollment verification form completed by your institution's registrar office — not by you. DGG will send Form B to your registrar after receiving your Form A or Form C. You should follow up with your registrar to make sure it's been returned. Without Form B, your enrollment status cannot be confirmed and funding cannot be calculated.
Why are my payments on hold? ▾
Payments go on hold if: (1) your void cheque has not been uploaded, (2) Form B has not been received from your institution, (3) your application has not yet been approved by the Director, or (4) you have outstanding reporting requirements. Check your notifications for the specific reason.
How do I claim travel expenses? ▾
Submit Form E within 30 days of travel. You must provide receipts — no receipts means no reimbursement. Air travel is reimbursed at the most economical fare from your institution to the nearest major airport to Deline. Land travel is reimbursed per NJC NT kilometre rates. Maximum: $2,000 without dependents / $3,500 with dependents per trip, up to 2 trips per year.
What if I switch from full-time to part-time? ▾
You must submit Form D (Change of Information) immediately. Your living allowance will be recalculated at the part-time rate. Failing to report an enrollment change can result in an overpayment, which you will be required to repay. If you have a documented disability that affects your course load, contact DGG staff about accommodation.
Can I receive funding from both C-DFN and DGGR at the same time? ▾
Yes — stacking C-DFN (PSSSP or UCEPP) and DGGR is permitted. However, if you are receiving GNWT SFA, you are not eligible for C-DFN tuition or living allowances (DGGR is unaffected). You must report any SFA funding on your application.
How do I appeal a funding decision? ▾
Submit Form H (Appeal Request). You must clearly explain the decision you are appealing and cite the specific section(s) of the Education Policy you are relying on. The Director or Appeal Committee will decide: Approved, Denied, or Further Information Required. For C-DFN stream appeals, the CEO may be the final decision-maker. For DGGR stream appeals, a DGGR official may be involved.
What is the Hardship Bursary and can I apply? ▾
The Hardship Bursary (max $500) provides emergency financial support. It is entirely discretionary — it requires you to be currently active and compliant with all reporting requirements (no missing documents, no outstanding Form C). You can apply at any time with no fixed deadline. Approval is not guaranteed.
